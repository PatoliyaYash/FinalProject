Frequently Asked Question (FAQs)

Are there places in the Park to store my belongings?

Convenient locker facilities are available at Canada's Wonderland, located at the Front Gate adjacent to the Canada's Wonderland Store and at the entrance of Splash Works. Locker rental fees for all-day access range from $17 to $30, depending on the size of the locker and the current season.

Please note that seasonal pricing may apply. While lockers provide a secure storage solution, Canada's Wonderland assumes no liability for items stored within. Guests are encouraged to choose the appropriate locker size, keep their locker receipt safe, and consider sharing lockers to split costs. Enjoy your visit with peace of mind knowing your belongings are safely stored away!

Am I permitted to bring my own food and beverages into the Park?

No outside food, beverages or coolers are allowed to be brought into Canada's Wonderland. However, baby food and formula, bottled water or refillable bottles/containers are permitted into the park. No glass containers are allowed. Guests with baskets, coolers and personal picnic items are welcome to use the public picnic pavilion located just outside our front gates.

Those with medical dietary needs will be considered. Guests are encouraged to visit our Special Dietary Needs webpage and/or contact Guest Services prior to their visit for more information about options available to guests.

Are strollers or wheelchairs available for rent?

For our guests' convenience, we offer single and double strollers for a one-day rental. All rentals are on a first-come basis and may not be reserved in advance. The rental fee for a single stroller is $17 plus tax and for a double stroller is $26 plus tax. A limited number of wheelchairs and electronic convenience vehicles (ECV) are available for a one-day rental. It is recommended that guests bring their own wheelchairs. Visit our rentals page to see the wheelchair options. Canada's Wonderland offers many accessible ways to navigate and enjoy the park, including our ride accommodations program. Please visit our Accessibility page for more details.
*Please note that electronic convenience vehicles (ECV) are not available during WinterFest.

What happens in bad weather?

Rides will close due to extreme temperatures and adverse weather conditions - particularly heavy rain, high winds and/or lightning. This is to ensure that the safety of all our guests is not compromised in unpredictable weather.

Please be assured that Canada's Wonderland makes every effort to reopen the rides as quickly and safely as possible after the adverse weather conditions cease. No refunds or rainchecks will be issued due to adverse weather conditions.
